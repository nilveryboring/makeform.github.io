# Embed Your Form

You can embed a Makeform form on any webpage. Add it directly inside the page, open it as a popup or slider, or let it fill a full page.

## How embedding works

Go to the <span className="text-cyan-500 font-bold bg-cyan-100 px-2 py-1 rounded">Share</span> tab of your published form and choose <span className="text-cyan-500 font-bold bg-cyan-100 px-2 py-1 rounded">Embed</span>.

{/* Screenshot TODO: Replace /images/embed_share_tab.webp with the published form Share tab showing where to open Embed. */}
<div className="shadow-2xl mt-12">![Share tab showing the Embed entry](/images/embed_share_tab.webp)</div>

A preview window opens with the available embed types.

Select an embed type, adjust the options, and use the preview to check how it will look on a webpage.

Use the copy option that matches your site:

- <span className="text-cyan-500 font-bold bg-cyan-100 px-2 py-1 rounded">Get the code</span> — Copy the full embed code when your website lets you paste embed code.
- <span className="text-cyan-500 font-bold bg-cyan-100 px-2 py-1 rounded">Copy embed link</span> — Copy only the embed URL when your site builder or no-code tool asks for a form link.

---

## Choose an embed type

There are four embed types:

| Type | Best for |
|------|----------|
| Standard | A form inside normal page content |
| Popup | A form that opens over your page |
| Slider | A form that slides in from the side |
| Full page | A form that fills a page or section |

<Callout type="info">
Form content changes update automatically in existing embeds. If you change embed options such as width, background, or opening behavior, copy the updated code from the Share tab again.
</Callout>

## Standard embed

The standard embed places the form directly inside your webpage content.

{/* Screenshot TODO: Replace /images/embed_form1.webp with the Share tab embed preview window with Standard selected. Show the options panel, preview area, and Get the code button. */}
<div className="shadow-2xl mt-12">![Standard embed options](/images/embed_form1.webp)</div>

Select <span className="text-cyan-500 font-bold bg-cyan-100 px-2 py-1 rounded">Standard</span>, adjust the options, copy the code, and paste it where the form should appear on your page.

### Options

- **Width** — Adjust the form width for your page layout.
- **Height** — Set a fixed height, or enable dynamic height so the embed automatically fits the form content.
- **Transparent background** — Remove the default form background so the form blends into your page.
- **Align content to the left** — Align embedded form content to the left instead of centering it.
- **Track form events** — Send form load and submission events to your website. See <Link href="/developer/embedded/form-event" className="underline">Handle Form Events</Link>.
- **Show 'Powered by' badge** — Show or hide the `Powered by Makeform` badge where your plan allows it. See <Link href="/features/remove-makeform-branding" className="underline">Remove Makeform Branding</Link>.

<Callout type="info">
For normal page content, use the Standard embed code from the Share tab. Use Full page only when the form should fill the page or a dedicated section.
</Callout>

## Popup embed

Popup embeds open the form over your page. Center popups focus attention on the form, while bottom-right popups keep more of the page visible.

{/* Screenshot TODO: Replace /images/embed_form2.webp with the Share tab embed preview window with Popup selected. Show popup-specific options and the preview area. */}
<div className="shadow-2xl mt-12">![Popup embed options](/images/embed_form2.webp)</div>

Select <span className="text-cyan-500 font-bold bg-cyan-100 px-2 py-1 rounded">Popup</span>, choose how the popup should open, copy the code, and paste it into your website.

### Options

- **Position** — Open the popup in the center of the page or in the bottom-right corner.
- **Show dark overlay** — Dim the page behind a bottom-right popup. Center popups always use an overlay.
- **Open (form trigger)** — Choose whether the popup opens from a button click, on page load, after a delay, on exit intent, or after scrolling.
- **Width** — Adjust the popup width on desktop.
- **Close after submit** — Close the popup automatically after a successful submission.
- **Align content to the left** — Align form content to the left instead of centering it.
- **Track form events** — Send popup open, close, load, and submission events to your website. See <Link href="/developer/embedded/form-event" className="underline">Handle Form Events</Link>.
- **Show loading screen** — Show or hide the loading screen while the popup loads. This is the same setting as <Link href="/guides/form-settings#show-loading-screen" className="underline">Show Loading Screen</Link> in Form Settings.
- **Show 'Powered by' badge** — Show or hide the `Powered by Makeform` badge where your plan allows it. See <Link href="/features/remove-makeform-branding" className="underline">Remove Makeform Branding</Link>.

For automatic opening, custom behavior, or developer setup, see <Link href="/developer/embedded/developer-resources" className="underline">Developer Resources</Link>.

## Slider embed

Slider embeds open the form as a side panel. They open from the right by default, but can also open from the left.

<div className="shadow-2xl mt-12">![Slider embed options](/images/embed_form3.webp)</div>

Select <span className="text-cyan-500 font-bold bg-cyan-100 px-2 py-1 rounded">Slider</span>, choose the side it should open from, copy the code, and paste it into your website.

### Options

- **Position** — Open the slider from the right or left side of the page.
- **Open (form trigger)** — Choose whether the slider opens from a button click, on page load, after a delay, on exit intent, or after scrolling.
- **Width** — Adjust the slider width on desktop.
- **Close after submit** — Close the slider automatically after a successful submission.
- **Align content to the left** — Align form content to the left instead of centering it.
- **Track form events** — Send slider open, close, load, and submission events to your website. See <Link href="/developer/embedded/form-event" className="underline">Handle Form Events</Link>.
- **Show loading screen** — Show or hide the loading screen while the slider loads. This is the same setting as <Link href="/guides/form-settings#show-loading-screen" className="underline">Show Loading Screen</Link> in Form Settings.
- **Show 'Powered by' badge** — Show or hide the `Powered by Makeform` badge where your plan allows it. See <Link href="/features/remove-makeform-branding" className="underline">Remove Makeform Branding</Link>.

For automatic opening, custom behavior, or developer setup, see <Link href="/developer/embedded/developer-resources" className="underline">Developer Resources</Link>.

## Full page embed

Use a full page embed when the form should be the main content of a page or fill a dedicated section.

{/* Screenshot TODO: Replace /images/embed_form4.webp with the Share tab embed preview window with Full page selected. Show any available options, the preview area, and the Get the code button. */}
<div className="shadow-2xl mt-12">![Full page embed options](/images/embed_form4.webp)</div>

Select <span className="text-cyan-500 font-bold bg-cyan-100 px-2 py-1 rounded">Full page</span>, copy the code, and paste it into the page or container where the form should fill the available space.

### Options

- **Transparent background** — Remove the default form background so the form blends into the page.
- **Track form events** — Send form load and submission events to your website. See <Link href="/developer/embedded/form-event" className="underline">Handle Form Events</Link>.
- **Show loading screen** — Show or hide the loading screen while the full page form loads. This is the same setting as <Link href="/guides/form-settings#show-loading-screen" className="underline">Show Loading Screen</Link> in Form Settings.
- **Show 'Powered by' badge** — Show or hide the `Powered by Makeform` badge where your plan allows it. See <Link href="/features/remove-makeform-branding" className="underline">Remove Makeform Branding</Link>.

## Hidden fields and locale

Pass hidden fields by adding query parameters to the embed URL:

```html
YOUR_EMBED_URL?user_id=123&utm_source=docs
```

Choose a locale in the Share tab if your embedded form should use a specific language. See <Link href="/guides/supported-languages" className="underline">Supported Languages</Link> for language options.

See <Link href="/features/hidden-fields" className="underline">Hidden Fields</Link> for setup and URL parameters, or <Link href="/developer/embedded/developer-resources" className="underline">Developer Resources</Link> for developer setup.

## Shared form settings

Some options in the embed panel are saved on the form itself and stay in sync with Form Settings:

| Setting | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| Show loading screen | Controls the form loading screen for popup, slider, and full page embeds. This is the same option as Show Loading Screen in Form Settings. |
| Custom loading text | Shows custom loading copy while the form loads. |
| Show 'Powered by' badge | Controls the `Powered by Makeform` badge. |
| Remove Makeform branding | Removes Makeform branding where your plan allows it. |
