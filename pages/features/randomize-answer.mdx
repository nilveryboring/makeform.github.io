# Randomize Answers

Randomize answer options to reduce order bias when respondents rank choices.

<Callout type="info">
Randomization is available on ranking questions. Each respondent sees the options in a shuffled order when the form loads.
</Callout>

## When to use randomization

Use randomized answers when the order of options could influence the response.

Good examples:

- Product preference tests
- Feature prioritization surveys
- Candidate or idea ranking
- Research studies where first-option bias matters

Do not randomize options when the order itself carries meaning, such as steps in a process, sizes from small to large, or time ranges.

## Turn on randomized answers

1. Open your form in the builder.
2. Add or select a <span className="text-cyan-500 font-semibold bg-cyan-100 px-2 py-1 rounded">Ranking</span> question.
3. Open the question settings.
4. Turn on <span className="text-cyan-500 font-semibold bg-cyan-100 px-2 py-1 rounded">Randomize options</span>.
5. Save or publish the form.

Randomization happens for respondents, not while you are editing the form. Use preview or a published form link to test the respondent experience.

## How results are saved

Makeform saves the selected option values, not their displayed position.

That means your results stay readable even when different respondents saw the same options in a different order.

## Use with partial ranking

Randomized answers work well with <span className="text-cyan-500 font-semibold bg-cyan-100 px-2 py-1 rounded">Allow partial ranking</span>.

For example, you can ask respondents to rank their top 3 options from a longer randomized list. This is useful when you want preference data without forcing people to rank every option.

## Testing checklist

- Preview the form more than once and confirm the option order changes.
- Submit a test response and confirm the ranked values are saved correctly.
- Keep option labels clear enough to make sense in any order.
- Turn randomization off for options that should stay in a fixed sequence.

## Troubleshooting

### The order does not change in the editor

That is expected. Randomization is designed for the respondent view.

### Results look out of order

The display order can vary by respondent. Read the selected option values, not the original option position.

### The setting is missing

Check that you are editing a ranking question. Randomized answers are not shown for every field type.
